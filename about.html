The Story

A musician’s ear.
An engineer’s standard.
A Nigerian workshop.

EL-NOVIK is not a store. It’s a workshop — founded by a musician who spent his life learning what makes sound feel right, and who refuses to sell anything he wouldn’t play himself.

The Journey

How a love for sound
became a standard.

I

A young ear, a self-taught hand.

The story begins early — with a Nigerian boy who fell in love with music before most kids learn to read sheet music. No formal teacher, no expensive instruments. Just a relentless ear and the discipline to teach himself. By the time others were starting lessons, he was already fluent.

II

Many instruments, one language.

Guitar. Keyboard. Traditional drums. Modern percussion. He didn’t pick one and call it home — he learned the language each instrument spoke. That fluency taught him something most musicians never learn: every instrument has a soul, and every soul demands a different kind of respect.

III

Trained abroad. Rooted at home.

The journey took him to the United Kingdom — not to leave Nigeria, but to learn what the rest of the world considered a standard. He studied, he played, he watched how serious sound engineers worked. Then he brought that standard home, because Nigerian music deserved the same.

IV

EL-NOVIK becomes a workshop.

What began as a personal practice became a brand for other musicians, churches, studios, and creators. EL-NOVIK was founded on a simple principle: never sell what you wouldn’t play yourself. Every instrument is chosen by someone who knows the difference. Every piece of electronics is tested by an ear, not just a meter.

What We Stand For

The promises
behind every product.

Tested by ear, not by spec sheet.

Numbers and specifications matter, but they don’t hear. Every instrument and every audio component we stock is played, tested, and judged by people who know what good sound feels like.

Built to last in real conditions.

Nigerian power. Nigerian humidity. Nigerian stages and Nigerian churches. We don’t recommend gear that can’t survive how it’s actually used here. If it can’t handle a Sunday service, we don’t sell it.

Advice over upsell.

We’d rather sell you the right thing once than the wrong thing twice. Tell us what you’re building and what your budget is — we’ll tell you honestly what fits, even when that means recommending something cheaper than you expected.

Relationships, not transactions.

We’re here after the sale, not just before it. Questions about setup, replacements, repairs, upgrades, recommendations — that’s the part of the job most stores skip. It’s the part that matters most to us.
